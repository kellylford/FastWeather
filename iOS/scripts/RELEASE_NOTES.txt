New in this build

Air Quality (US cities): City detail now has an Air Quality section for US locations. It leads with the observed air quality index and category (Good, Moderate, Unhealthy, and so on) from EPA AirNow ground monitors, followed by plain-language health guidance and a pollutant breakdown. When an official air quality alert is active for the city, the section shows it and defers to it — so the reading reflects real conditions (for example, wildfire-smoke events) rather than a forecast model that can lag.

The section is fully VoiceOver-labeled: the category word and health guidance carry the meaning, not color. You can reorder or hide it like any other detail section (Settings, then the detail section list), and it can be toggled under Developer Settings, "Air Quality Section."

For now this is US-only, since the observed data and alerts are US sources. International support will come once it can use the correct regional index.

What to test
- Open a US city and find the Air Quality section (just under Weather Alerts).
- Check that the number, category, and health guidance read correctly with VoiceOver.
- If your area has an active air quality alert, confirm it appears and opens full details.
- Confirm non-US cities do not show the section.
